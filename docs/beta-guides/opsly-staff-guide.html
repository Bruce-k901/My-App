Getting Started

1. Setting Up Your Account

Your manager will add you to the system and you'll receive an email from Opsly to set up your account.

1
Check your email

Look for an invitation email from Opsly. Click the setup link inside.

2
Create your password

Choose a password you'll remember (minimum 6 characters). There's a password generator button if you want a suggestion.

3
Set your 4-digit PIN

Choose a PIN you'll remember — you'll use this for quick actions like completing tasks and clocking in.

4
Log in

Go to your Opsly URL and sign in with your email and password. You'll see the home screen with your tasks and schedule.

Getting Started

2. Finding Your Way Around

On Your Phone (recommended for daily use)

The bottom of the screen has 5 tabs:

Tab What It Does
Home Your dashboard — weather, priority items, upcoming tasks, activity feed
Tasks Today's compliance tasks that need completing (Checkly)
Calendar Your shifts and leave in calendar view
Rota The weekly rota showing your shifts
More Quick actions (clock in, report incident, temp check) and links to everything else

Tap your avatar (top-right) to access your profile, switch sites (if you work at more than one), change theme, or log out.

On Desktop

Use the Module Bar at the top to switch between Checkly (tasks) and Teamly (shifts/leave). A sidebar on the left shows navigation for whichever module you're in.

3. Your Profile & Settings

You can update your personal details at any time:

  • Phone: Tap avatar → Settings → Profile
  • Password: Settings → Profile → Change Password
  • Theme: Switch between dark mode and light mode in Settings → Appearance (or via the theme toggle in the header)
  • Notifications: Choose which email alerts you receive in Settings → Notifications
Note: Your name, email, job title, and site assignment are managed by your manager. If any of these are wrong, let your manager know.

4. Installing on Your Phone

Opsly works best when installed as an app on your phone. Here's how:

iPhone (Safari)

  1. Open Opsly in Safari
  2. Tap the Share button (square with arrow)
  3. Scroll down and tap “Add to Home Screen”
  4. Tap “Add”

Android (Chrome)

  1. Open Opsly in Chrome
  2. Tap the three dots menu (top-right)
  3. Tap “Add to Home screen” or “Install app”
  4. Tap “Install”

Once installed, Opsly will open full-screen like a regular app — no browser bars.

Checkly — Your Daily Tasks

5. Viewing Today's Tasks

Your daily compliance tasks are in the Tasks tab (mobile) or under Checkly › Today's Tasks (desktop).

Tasks are organised by time of day:

Daypart Time Examples
Morning 5am – 12pm Opening checks, morning temperature logs
Afternoon 12pm – 5pm Midday temperature checks, cleaning tasks
Evening 5pm – 10pm Evening checks, close-down tasks
Night 10pm – 5am End-of-night cleaning, security checks

Each task card shows the task name, category, and due time. Tasks that are due soon are shown first.

Note: You only see tasks that are due around now. Tasks for later in the day will appear when it's time to do them.
Checkly — Your Daily Tasks

6. Completing a Task

Tap on any task card to open it. The completion form will guide you through what needs to be done.

1
Read the instructions

At the top of the form, you'll see instructions explaining what to check and how.

2
Work through each item

The form will have a mix of items depending on the task:

  • Checklist items — tick each one as you complete it
  • Yes / No questions — answer honestly (e.g. “Is the hand wash basin stocked?”)
  • Temperature readings — enter the reading from your thermometer (see Section 7)
  • Photos — take a photo as evidence (see Section 8)
  • Notes — add any observations or comments
3
Submit

Once all required fields are completed, tap Submit. The task will move to the Completed section. Your name and the time are recorded automatically.

Tip: The system tracks how long each task takes (from when you open it to when you submit). This is just for reporting — take the time you need to do it properly.
Checkly — Your Daily Tasks

7. Temperature Checks

For temperature tasks, you'll see a list of equipment (e.g. “Walk-in Fridge”, “Freezer 1”, “Hot Hold Unit”). For each one:

1
Take the reading

Use your probe thermometer or check the unit's display.

2
Enter the temperature

Type the reading into the field. The system will immediately show you whether it's In Range (green) or Out of Range (red).

3
If it's out of range

Don't panic. Record the actual reading — the system will handle the rest. It may ask you to re-check later or your manager will be notified. Add a note explaining anything relevant (e.g. “door was left open, closed and will recheck in 30 mins”).

Important: Always record the actual temperature, even if it's out of range. Never “fudge” a reading — the system is designed to help you manage breaches, not punish them. Honest recording protects you and the business.
Checkly — Your Daily Tasks

8. Taking Photo Evidence

Some tasks require photo evidence (e.g. cleaning completion, probe calibration, delivery checks).

  • Tap the camera icon or “Add Photo” button
  • Take a clear photo showing what's being evidenced
  • You can add multiple photos if needed
  • Photos are uploaded and stored securely — they're visible to managers in the audit trail
Tip: Make sure photos are clear and well-lit. A blurry or dark photo won't serve as good evidence if needed for an audit.
Checkly — Your Daily Tasks

9. Flagging Issues

If you spot a problem while completing a task, you can flag it:

  • Look for the flag option on the completion form
  • Turn it on and enter a brief reason (e.g. “grease trap needs emptying”, “fire exit blocked by delivery”)
  • You can still submit the task — the flag alerts your manager that something needs attention

Flagged tasks are highlighted in the completed tasks list and in reports, so your manager can follow up.

Teamly — Your Shifts & Time Off

10. Viewing Your Rota

Your shifts are in the Rota tab (mobile) or under Teamly › Schedule › Rota (desktop).

The rota shows a weekly view with your shifts for each day. Each shift shows:

  • Start time and finish time
  • Break duration
  • Which section/role you're covering
  • Any notes from your manager

You can also see your shifts in the Calendar tab alongside any approved leave.

Note: You'll receive a notification when your manager publishes a new rota. Until it's published, shifts are in draft and may change.
Teamly — Your Shifts & Time Off

11. Clocking In & Out

Clock in when you arrive for your shift and out when you leave.

On mobile:

1
Tap “More” (bottom tab)

The Clock In button is one of the priority actions at the top.

2
Tap “Clock In”

Your clock-in time is recorded. The button changes to “Clock Out”.

3
Clock out at the end of your shift

Tap “Clock Out” when your shift ends. Your hours are recorded for the timesheet.

On desktop:

The Clock In button is in the Module Bar (top-right, always visible).

Don't forget to clock out! If you forget, your manager will need to manually adjust your timesheet. Get into the habit of clocking out before you leave.
Teamly — Your Shifts & Time Off

12. Setting Your Availability

Teamly › Schedule › Availability

Let your manager know when you can and can't work:

Recurring availability (your regular pattern):

Set your general weekly pattern, e.g. “available Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm”. This repeats every week and helps your manager build the rota.

Specific date overrides:

Need a specific day off or have a commitment on a particular date? Add a date-specific override, e.g. “unavailable Saturday 8th March”.

You can also mark preferred times and set a maximum hours per day.

Note: Availability changes may need manager approval. Once submitted, they'll appear as pending until approved.

13. Requesting Leave

Teamly › Leave › Request Leave
1
Choose the leave type

Select from: Annual Leave, Sick Leave, TOIL, Unpaid Leave, Compassionate Leave, or others as configured by your manager.

2
Select your dates

Pick the start and end dates. Half-days are available for some leave types.

3
Check your balance

The system shows your remaining leave balance before you submit, so you know exactly how many days you have left.

4
Add a reason (optional)

You can add a note for your manager if needed.

5
Submit

Your request will be sent to your manager for approval. You'll be notified when it's approved or declined.

Teamly — Your Shifts & Time Off

14. Checking Your Leave Balance

Teamly › Leave › Balances

View how many leave days you have:

  • Entitled — your total annual allowance
  • Carried over — days carried from last year
  • Taken — days already used
  • Pending — days requested but not yet approved
  • Remaining — days still available

You can also see the Team Calendar (Teamly › Leave › Team Calendar) to see when your colleagues are off — helpful for planning your own requests.

15. Your Training Records

Teamly › Training

Your training and certification status is tracked in the app. You can view:

  • Which courses you've completed
  • Certificate expiry dates
  • Which courses are due for renewal

Categories tracked include: Food Safety, Health & Safety, Fire Marshal, First Aid, and COSHH/Allergen Awareness.

Note: Your manager records training completions for you. If you've completed a course and it's not showing, let your manager know so they can log it.
